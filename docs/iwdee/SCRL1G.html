Area of Effect: 30-ft. radius

Fireball is one of the reasons that wizards gained acceptance in the field of adventuring. To cast it, the wizard points  finger and speaks the range at which the fireball is to burst. A streak flashes from the pointing digit and blossoms into a fireball, delivering fire damage proportional to the level of the wizard who cast it: 1d6 points of damage for each level of the spellcaster (up to a maximum of 10d6). Those who roll successful a Save vs. Spell manage to dodge, fall flat, or roll aside, taking only half damage.
